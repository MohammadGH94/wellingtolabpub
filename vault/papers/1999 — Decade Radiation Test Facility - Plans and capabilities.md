## Abstract
> Reconstructed from OpenAlex inverted index — word order is correct, punctuation may be approximate.

The Decade Radiation Test Facility (DRTF) is located at the Arnold Engineering Development Center (AEDC), Arnold Air Force Base, TN. This facility represents the most advanced generation of technology and is designed to test 21Sf century space and missile systems and their components against nuclear weapons effects (NWE). The DRTF has been developed as the first DOD nuclear weapons effects testing facility designed and built from the ground up to service the user community.’ The facility became operational during FY98 with the high-fidelity Modular Bremsstrahlung Source (MBS), and has demonstrated this user-friendly capability to satisfied customers. The first DecadeQuad operating in the bremsstrahlung mode was deployed in FY99, and the Plasma Radiation Source (PRS) will be deployed in FYOO. The Air Force, Defense Threat Reduction Agency (DTRA), and the Army have plans to provide significant additional operational capabilities, including large-area hot and cold X-ray capability utilizing two groups of modules (Quads), prompt gamma radiation capability, and debris gamma and beta radiation capability. Upgrade plans call for a high dose, high dose rate, and high-fidelity bremsstrahlung capability. Also, an advanced test bed consisting of a cryogenic vacuum chamber with visible and infrared (IR) scene generation and nuclear clutter simulation will be added. Studies into the addition of neutron guns are also being conducted. The DRTF will be the only Department of Defense (DOD) above-ground simulator capable of testing entire large-area operating electronic ensembles such as satellite surveillance, communication, and missile navigation subsystems. This modern test facility will have the capability to demonstrate that systems can meet their high confidence requirements and will survive and function in prompt radiation environments that might be experienced by the systems during their combat mission.
