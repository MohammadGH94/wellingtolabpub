## Abstract
> Reconstructed from OpenAlex inverted index — word order is correct, punctuation may be approximate.

Soluble Aβ oligomers mediate most synaptotoxic and neurotoxic effects of Aβ in AD. We have shown that the mouse monoclonal antibody mAb5E3 generated against the structurally constrained tripeptide cSNK specifically recognizes synthetic and native Aβ oligomers, but not SNK in fibrils, monomers or amyloid precursor protein, and also neutralizes Aβ oligomer toxicity. We now determine the proportion of mAb-5E3-type immunoreactivity in polyclonal sera from cSNK-immunized mice. Four mice were immunized subcutaneously every 3 weeks with cSNK peptide conjugated to keyhole limpet hemocyanin (KLH) + Emulsigen adjuvant, and sera collected by tail vein phlebotomy after 9 weeks. Using a Biacore™ 3000, we immobilized equal densities of cyclic or linear SNK conjugated to BSA, Aβ40 monomer and BSA (reference surface) on 4 separate flow cells of a biosensorchip. Immunized mouse serum, normal mouse serum, mAb5E3 and 2 commercially-available antibodies that bind Aβ peptide (mAb6E10 and mAb4G8) were then diluted, sequentially injected over the different immobilized peptides and antibody binding monitored in real time. In another set of experiments, we compared the binding of these antibodies to an Aβ42 oligomer preparation (containing monomers, low and high molecular weight oligomers) and to Aβ40 monomers. Immunized mice exhibited 6-fold and 34-fold higher binding to cSNK than to linear SNK and to Aβ monomer, respectively. Normal mouse serum demonstrated no binding to cyclic or linear SNK, but bound to Aβ40 monomers at levels similar to the immune serum. mAb5E3 bound with a high magnitude to cSNK, but did not bind to either linear SNK, nor Aβ monomer. Immune serum and mAb5E3 bound 4-fold and 8-fold higher respectively, to the Aβ42 oligomers than to the Aβ40 monomers. mAb6E10 and mAb4G8 showed no binding to cyclic or linear SNK, which is not contained in their cognate epitopes, but bound strongly to the Aβ monomer. Our study reveals that active immunization with the conformationally-constrained cSNK peptide generates antibodies that selectively recognize the cSNK oligomer-specific epitope, while sparing SNK recognition in other Aβ contexts, suggesting that the cyclic peptide could be a suitable immunogen for oligomer-specific therapeutic or prophylactic vaccines for Alzheimer's disease.
