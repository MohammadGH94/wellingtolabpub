## Abstract
> Reconstructed from OpenAlex inverted index — word order is correct, punctuation may be approximate.

Oligomers of the amyloid-beta (Aβ) peptide play key roles in neurotoxicity and region-to-region spreading of Alzheimer's disease (AD) neuropathology. We have previously presented data describing a novel immunological epitope that is accessible to antibody binding only when Aβ is in an oligomeric form. The oligomer-specific epitope consists of a constrained turn at residues 26-28: cyclized serine-asparagine-lysine (cSNK). We now present data indicating that 1) cSNK epitope is specific to toxic oligomers; 2) that passive infusion of cSNK antibodies reduces brain and CSF Aβ oligomers; and 3) cSNK active immunization generates an immune response to Aβ oligomers. Specificity of a murine monoclonal antibody raised against the cSNK epitope, 5E3, was evaluated by immunoelectron microscopy, surface plasmon resonance, dot blotting/immunoblotting, immunohistochemistry, and atomic force microscopy. Therapeutic potential of the 5E3 antibody was assessed in in vivo assays of oligomer behavioral toxicity and acute treatment of aged AD mouse models. Immune responses elicited by cSNK vaccine formulations were evaluated in young mice. cSNK vaccination was assessed in AD mouse models treated at 3 months of age with monthly vaccination and then evaluated at 13 months of age. A monoclonal antibody raised against this novel epitope (5E3) binds to synthetic Aβ oligomers (AβO), but is unreactive to monomeric and fibrillar Aβ, as well as to amyloid plaques. 5E3 is effective at blocking AβO behavioural toxicity in vivo in wild type mice. Moreover acute treatment with 5E3 reduced AβO concentration in CSF and brains of aged APP/PS1 and Tg2576 mice, with no apparent impact on pre-existing plaques. Furthermore, cSNK-vaccinated mice produced an AβO specific immune response, without cross-reactivity to Aβ monomers. These experiments demonstrate three important findings: 1) murine 5E3 can neutralize the toxicity of synthetic AβOs in the molecular weight range of AβOs previously implicated in synaptic dysfunction; 2) systemically administered 5E3 demonstrates target engagement for AβOs in two mouse models of Alzheimer disease in vivo; and 3) active vaccination with the cSNK epitope generates AβO reactivity in mice without recognition of other Aβ molecular species. Together, our data demonstrates that cSNK is a well-defined AβO-specific epitope with therapeutic potential.
